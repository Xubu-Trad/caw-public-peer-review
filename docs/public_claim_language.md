# Public Claim Language

Use these exact claim forms for public posts and articles.

## Safe and accurate

GilgameshCaw/Caw is a serious public build under review.

Current evidence does not prove it is official CAW.

Current evidence does not prove cawmmunity acceptance.

Current evidence does not prove manifesto-complete decentralization.

Testsite usage exists, but usage is not acceptance.

Public GitHub activity exists, but commits are not cawmmunity approval.

Renouncing one owner address is not enough; every owner, admin, proxy, config, LayerZero/OApp, frontend, API, indexer, validator, and operator path must be reviewed.

Independent historical recovery is not proven until a clean empty-database rebuild from public chain data produces deterministic export hashes.

Peer review must happen before manifesto-aligned acceptance, not after.

## Avoid unless direct proof exists

Do not claim Gilgamesh is proven scammer.

Do not claim criminal intent.

Do not claim nobody uses the testsite.

Do not claim LayerZero makes decentralization impossible in every design.

Do not claim cawmmunity rejection as a fact.

Do not claim final failure before reviewing final deployed bytecode.

## Strongest summary

Real build. Real usage. Missing final proof.

Show the contracts.
Verify the bytecode.
Close the control paths.
Prove the rebuild.
Review in public.

## Public trail / governance-context language

Safe wording:

Gilgamesh's public trail is relevant to the review because the manifesto warns about people claiming ownership of a leaderless process.

Public statements about being a dev, referring to Maker, or using leader-style language should be treated as governance-context evidence, not as bytecode proof.

The issue is not that someone built code.

The issue is whether the build process, authority posture, and final technical architecture match a manifesto that rejects centralized control.

Do not ignore the public trail.

Do not overstate it.

Use it as context, then require technical proof.
